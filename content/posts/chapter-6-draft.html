--- title: "Gros Morne Justice: Chapter 6" date: 2015-08-26 02:28:00 tags: [] categories: ["Uncategorized"] ---   A line from which there can be no return The media is abuzz with bear attack news. One victim of the bear attacks was “Jessie Black of Deer Lake, the reigning queen of the Lomond River Golf Club’s par-three sixth hole.” What. What does that mean. One newspaper reports “BAD-ASSED BEAR NEWS: IT’S BAD, REAL BAD” reporting, “This is the most serious blow to the industry since the 9/11 terrorist attack. Locals have reported cancellations of ten family fishing trips up the Humber River this summer.” The parks officials are trying to hush up and downplay the severity of the bear attacks.   Rick preston along with some other hunters killed seven of the bears. He’s basically a hero. Jack feels defiled by the injustice of this.   Norm hires a new employee for Jack to show around, Mark Hughes. After Jack takes Mark to the cemetery and gets him set up digging some graves, Jack takes off to go feed his bear. The bear suddenly tenses, charges off, and Jack hears a scream. Starlight has pinned Mark Hughes to the ground. Jack says “NO” and the bear releases Mark. Mark explains that Norm had asked him to follow Jack to see where he goes every afternoon. He also helpfully points out that feeding bears isn’t legal. He says that he won’t say anything about what Jack’s been doing, but Jack doesn’t trust him. Jack says “Get him!”, but waffles on his decision and says “No” once Starlight begins attacking again. Starlight does not stop until Mark is dead.   Jack returns Mark to the cemetery, and parades Starlight around, so it will look as if Mark was killed in a bear attack at the cemetery. Jack notes to himself, that maybe he is crossing a line of no return.   Assorted thoughts
  • From the beginning of the chapter “Jack studied the accompanying photo. Yes, Kim Hollings, that was her name. She was trim, black-haired, pretty, with a rosy robust face. She wore her ranger uniform and hat, the epitome of efficiency and detachment. He wondered if she was married.” I don’t really know what to say about that paragraph, but I think Robbie sums it up best with his response of “This is a really nice book.”
  • We finally got a hint of the 9/11 connection that we’ve been waiting for since the celebrity review.
  • I had initially assumed, just based on every single indication of what kind of book this is, that Jack was going to be the archetypical rugged hero-protagonist. But he basically just murdered some kid in order to cover his tracks...the tracks where his habit of feeding human flesh to bears has coincided with a drastic increase in bear attacks on humans. Jack is a full on anti-hero at best. This is a very gritty and sophisticated story!
  • “As he put his plan into motion, somewhere in the back of his mind he registered a detached awareness that he had crossed a line from which there can be no return.” This, in response to commanding your attack bear to attack and kill someone and choosing to try to cover up the death.
  • How does Jack get Starlight to the cemetery? Does he just drive a bear around town in his truck??? The book offers no explanation.
  • This chapter, a montage of bear killings as reported in the newspapers, was called “Marking Territory”. The next chapter is called “When Bears Attack”. Just how much is this going to ramp up?
  Updated Plot Predictions
  • Norm and Jack are now explicitly enemies, since Norm sent Mark to spy on Jack. What were Norm’s suspicions?
  • Jack has successfully trained his bear to attack people, but can’t make the bear stop attacking on command. It seems that Jack’s army is a powerful, but unpredictable, weapon. Is this novel a metaphor for Mutually Assured Destruction?

  • Will Dorothy be eaten by a bear? Will Jack allow, or maybe even command this? “Get her”. I feel like this is the farthest that Jack can fall, but this book always seems to defy our imaginations.
